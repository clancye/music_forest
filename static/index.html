why this record?

This record shares today's date — any year. Music Forest isn't recommending it: it gathers every record released on this day and shows them one at a time. The running order is balanced across genres so the day stays varied — never tailored to you, and nothing about you is stored or read to build it. You can narrow the day to the genres you choose under By genre.

how, exactly?

Every album in the catalogue is tagged with its original release month and day. Music Forest gathers every record whose release date lands on today's month and day — any year — and shows them one at a time.

The order is a genre-balanced deal: records are grouped by a coarse genre and the deck takes one genre per round, so the first records span genres instead of leading with a run of the most common one. Within that, placement is random. Genre is the only thing balanced — to keep the day varied — and there's no weighting by popularity, era, label, or anything about you; nothing about you is stored or read to build the day. Only one canonical version of each album is counted, so reissues and duplicate pressings can't crowd the day or show up twice.

Prefer a narrower day? By genre shows only the genres you choose — and the filtered set still runs through the same balanced deal, so "Jazz + Folk" interleaves rather than all-jazz-then-folk.

why keep?

Keeping saves a record to your Notebook — a private, end-to-end- encrypted field notebook of your listening life. Write whatever you want and it's yours to return to, any time. Music Forest never recommends, ranks, or shares; the notebook is only ever for you.

Where the data comes from

Discogs — one of two catalog sources: releases, dates, labels and editions, from its CC0 community data.

MusicBrainz — the other catalog source, global and an equal peer (nearly half the pickable pool comes from it), and where many albums' tracklists, genres and cover art come from.

Deezer — the availability check: an album only counts as listenable once it's been confirmed streamable there.

Apple Music / iTunes & the Cover Art Archive — cover art, plus exact Apple album links.

Spotify & YouTube Music — exact album links from each service's own catalog lookup (Spotify's cached only briefly, per its terms).

Bandcamp — exact album links drawn straight from MusicBrainz's url-relations, so a Bandcamp album is confirmed by construction — no lookup needed.

Odesli — fans a confirmed album out to exact links on further services (TIDAL, Amazon Music, Pandora and more).

The house rule: a link or badge only appears when the exact album was confirmed on that service. An album that hasn't been checked yet is unknown — never "unavailable."
