Privacy Policy

Your notebook is yours, and no one else can read it.

Effective date: July 11, 2026

Music Forest is a personal field notebook for your listening life — records released on this date, any year, and a private place to write. It is not a recommender, an ad product, or an engagement machine, and this policy reflects that: we collect as little as possible, we don't track you, and the notebook you write is end-to-end encrypted so that we cannot read it.

This policy covers the hosted service at musicforest.lol.

The short version

  • Your notebook is end-to-end encrypted. Your notes, choices, trails, and platform marks are encrypted on your device with a key derived from your passphrase. We store only encrypted data and cannot read it. Your passphrase and encryption keys never leave your device.
  • We don't track you. No advertising, no analytics, no third-party trackers, no behavioral profiling, no selling or sharing of your data. Ever.
  • We keep almost no logs about you. Our application logs don't record your IP address or anything you write.
  • You're in control. You can export your entire notebook at any time, and you can ask us to delete your account and data.
  • The one thing we can read is feedback you deliberately send us — because you asked us to look at it.

1Who we are

The service is operated by Clancy Emanuel (operating as Music Forest). For any privacy question, or to exercise the rights below, contact us at info@musicforest.lol.

2What we collect, and why

a) Account information

To sign in you provide an email address and a password, or you sign in with Google, or you use a one-time email sign-in link. We use this only to authenticate you and to contact you about the service (for example, an invite or an account notice). Authentication is handled by our provider, Supabase (and Google, if you choose Google sign-in). We do not receive or store your password in readable form.

b) Your notebook (end-to-end encrypted)

The notes, choices, trails, and platform availability marks you create are encrypted on your device before they reach us. We store only the encrypted result and the timestamps needed to sync it across your devices. Because the encryption key is derived from your passphrase and never leaves your device, we cannot read your notebook, and neither can our hosting or database providers. The "connections" the app surfaces from your notes are computed on your device and are never stored or transmitted.

c) Feedback you send us

If you use the "Send feedback" button, we receive your message and — to help us fix what you're reporting — a snapshot of your current screen (a screenshot and a copy of the page), plus basic technical details (browser type, screen/window size, language, and the in-app URL). This is the one place your content is readable by us, and it is only ever sent when you click Send. Please note the screenshot and page snapshot capture whatever is on your screen at that moment — if you are viewing your notebook, that text may be included — so avoid sending feedback from a screen you'd rather we not see.

d) Access requests

If the service is invite-only and you ask for an invite, we collect the email address and any note you submit, plus your browser's User-Agent, so we can review and respond. We use this only to manage invitations.

e) Technical and security data

Like any website, our hosting provider processes standard request information, including your IP address, to deliver and secure the service. We use your IP address transiently and in memory to rate-limit abuse of public endpoints; our own application logs record only non-identifying request metadata (the request method, the path without its query string, a status code, timing, an opaque request identifier, and your opaque account identifier) and do not record your IP address or anything you write.

We do not: run advertising or ad trackers; use analytics or behavioral profiling; build a profile of your tastes to "recommend" things; or sell, rent, or share your personal information with anyone for their own purposes.

3Cookies and local storage

We don't use advertising or tracking cookies. The app stores a few things in your own browser to function: your sign-in session, your on-device preferences (such as which streaming services to show and your auto-lock timing), and — if you opt in to "stay unlocked on this device" — a device-trust record protected by your device's biometrics or PIN. The app also caches its own code so it works offline. None of this is sent to us as tracking, and you can clear it at any time by signing out and clearing your browser's site data.

The Service is operated from and intended for users in the United States. Our hosting is in the United States (Render, Oregon) and our database/auth provider stores data in the United States (Supabase, AWS us-east-2, Ohio). If you access the Service from outside the United States, you do so on your own initiative and consent to your information being processed in the United States.

6How long we keep data

  • Your encrypted notebook and keys: kept until you delete the items or your account.
  • Account information: kept while your account exists.
  • Feedback: kept while we work on what you reported, then deleted — no later than six months after you sent it.
  • Access requests: deleted within six months of our decision, and no later than twelve months after your last request even if we never got to it.
  • Rate-limit data: transient, in memory only.
  • Hosting logs: retained by our hosting provider per its standard practice.

7Your rights and choices

Whatever your location, you can:

  • Export your entire notebook at any time from within the app (it decrypts on your device and downloads as a portable file).
  • Delete individual notebook entries in the app.
  • Delete your account and associated data from the account menu ("Delete account"), which irreversibly erases your encrypted notebook + keys and removes your login/email.
  • Clear local data by signing out and clearing your browser's site data.

Depending on where you live, you may also have rights to access, correct, restrict or object to processing, and to data portability, and the right to withdraw consent and to lodge a complaint with your data protection authority. To exercise any of these, contact us at info@musicforest.lol. We won't discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

Note on our ability to help with access/correction requests: because your notebook is end-to-end encrypted, we can't read, retrieve, or correct its contents for you — only you can, using the in-app export and editing tools. This is a privacy feature, not an evasion. We can delete the encrypted data and your account on request.

11Security

Your notebook is protected with end-to-end encryption (AES-GCM, with keys derived from your passphrase using Argon2id); we hold only ciphertext and cannot decrypt it. We serve the app over HTTPS with a strict Content-Security-Policy and related protections, pin our third-party scripts with Subresource Integrity, and guard server-side fetches against abuse. No system is perfectly secure, but we design to hold as little readable data as possible. When you set up your notebook you are shown a recovery code (once, at signup): either your passphrase or that code can unlock your notebook, and the code is the reset path if you forget your passphrase — we store only wrapped keys we cannot open. Important: if you lose both your passphrase and your recovery code (and haven't enabled device unlock), your encrypted notebook cannot be recovered — by you or by us.
